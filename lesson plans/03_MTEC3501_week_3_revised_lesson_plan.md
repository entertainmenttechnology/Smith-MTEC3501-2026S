### üü£ Part 4 ‚Äì Mini-Lecture: Brainstorming as Method (15 min)

**Key Framing:**
- Brainstorming is not a personality trait; it is a **set of techniques**
- Different techniques produce different kinds of information
- Earlier work relied on **spontaneous ideation**
- Today‚Äôs focus: **directed ideation**

Introduce the idea that structured constraints often *increase* creativity by making thinking visible and transferable.

## üü† Part 5 ‚Äì Directed Brainstorming Roundtable (40 min)

**Purpose:**
- Replace open-ended ‚Äúguided questions‚Äù with explicit method rehearsal
- Allow students to *experience* multiple brainstorming techniques
- Build a shared vocabulary around ideation tools

**Structure:**
- One student becomes the focus at a time
- One brainstorming technique is applied per student
- The class participates collectively
- The focused student listens and takes notes only
- No defending, correcting, or narrowing during the exercise

> Instructor framing to students:
> *‚ÄúWe are not trying to solve your project today. We are testing tools and observing what kinds of insights they generate.‚Äù*
