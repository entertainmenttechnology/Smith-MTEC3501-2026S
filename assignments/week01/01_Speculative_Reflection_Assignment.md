# MTEC 3501 - Assignment 01: Speculative Reflection (Version 1)

Due: Week 2 (before class)  
Format: Shareable Google Doc, Dropbox link, or PDF (no GitHub upload yet)  
Length: 1-2 pages, or equivalent media with captions/notes  
In-class: 2-3 minute oral framing

## Learning Goal
By the end of this assignment, you should be able to articulate one bold but plausible project concept as a clear user experience and identify its most important unknowns.

## Assignment Prompt
Develop a first-pass reflection on a long-term project idea drawn from your own experiences, skills, passions, and inspirations.

Rules for this stage:
1. No outside research yet. Use your own thinking and prior knowledge.
2. Be ambitious about impact and scale.
3. Stay physically plausible (no ideas that require breaking known physical laws).
4. Focus on user experience: what participants do, sense, and feel.
5. Surface open questions and perceived difficulties honestly.

This submission is Version 1. It is intentionally exploratory and will be revised after feedback.

## Required Deliverables
Submit one shareable artifact that includes all six items below.

1. Working title and one-sentence concept
What good looks like:
- The title is specific, memorable, and not generic.
- The one-sentence concept clearly states who the experience is for and what happens.

2. Experience scenario (150-250 words, or equivalent storyboard/audio script)
What good looks like:
- Describes the modality (game, installation, performance, music system, robot, hybrid, etc.).
- Explains what the participant does step-by-step.
- Conveys what the participant feels or notices over time.

3. Speculative value statement (100-150 words)
What good looks like:
- Explains why the idea is bold, emergent, or potentially disruptive.
- Distinguishes this concept from common or already-saturated approaches.

4. Personal grounding statement (75-150 words)
What good looks like:
- Connects the idea to your lived experience, prior work, or long-term goals.
- Explains why you are the right person (or team lead) to explore it.

5. Challenges and unknowns list (minimum 5 bullets)
What good looks like:
- Includes technical, experiential, and production risks.
- Uses concrete questions, not vague concerns.

6. Version 2 plan (minimum 3 revision actions)
What good looks like:
- Lists exactly what you will refine after in-class feedback.
- Each action is measurable (for example, "narrow audience from everyone to first-year commuters").

## Checkpoints and Milestones
1. Week 1 checkpoint (end of week): Draft title + one-sentence concept + tentative modality.
2. Week 2 pre-class submission: Full Version 1 artifact submitted.
3. Week 2 in-class: 2-3 minute oral framing and peer feedback.
4. Week 2 post-class: Start Version 2 revision plan (used in next assignment).

## Oral Framing Requirements (2-3 minutes)
Address these points in order:
1. What is the experience?
2. Who is it for?
3. What does the participant do and feel?
4. What are your top unknowns?
5. What will you revise next?

## Scope Boundaries
Out of scope for Assignment 01:
1. Full technical architecture or implementation plan.
2. Formal literature review or citation requirements.
3. Claims that depend on impossible science.
4. Overly broad targets such as "for everyone" without a clear audience slice.

## Grading Rubric (100 points)
1. Specificity and feasibility of concept: 30
2. User experience clarity and modality definition: 25
3. Depth of challenges and unknowns: 20
4. Quality of revision plan (Version 1 to Version 2): 15
5. Professionalism, completeness, and submission quality: 10

## Quick Quality Reference
Strong Version 1 tends to:
1. Name a specific user context.
2. Describe a concrete interaction sequence.
3. Admit real risks and open questions.
4. Propose clear next-step revisions.

Weak Version 1 tends to:
1. Stay abstract ("an app that helps people") without experience details.
2. Avoid uncertainty or risk discussion.
3. Promise outcomes without describing interaction.

## FAQ
1. Can this become a team project later?
Yes. For now, submit individually and note possible collaborator roles if relevant.

2. Can I include sketches, audio, or video instead of only text?
Yes. Mixed media is welcome, but all required deliverable items must still be clearly addressed.

3. Do I need citations in Assignment 01?
No. Research and citation structure begin in later assignments.

4. How polished does this need to be?
It should be clear, complete, and professionally formatted, but it is still exploratory Version 1.
