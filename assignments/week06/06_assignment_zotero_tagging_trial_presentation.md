## Non-Negotiables

- Be specific. Vagueness is not acceptable.
- If something is unresolved, name it directly.
- Do not leave structural gaps. Replace blank sections with a defined open question.
- **Do not read from slides.** Slides are structure support, not script pages.
- Keep the practice presentation under 10 minutes.

If you are stuck between options, document:

1. The options
2. Decision criteria
3. Test/research needed to decide
4. Where this decision appears in your process diagram
5. It is better to select one and move on (documenting this is still an unknown). Often deploying one solution will allow testing or better evaluation.

## Key Terms (Use Consistently)

- **Climax:** your full, ideal project vision.
- **LVP (Least Viable Product):** the leanest version that still expresses the core experience/mechanism.
- **PoC (Proof of Concept):** the end-of-semester demonstrable build that proves your core system works.
- **Breakpoint ("Miracle")**: a large unresolved leap in logic, production, or feasibility.

## Part I – Zotero Tagging (Required)

Continue building and structuring your Zotero library. This week formalizes your tagging standard.

Tagging guide: [Mastering Zotero: Working with Tags](https://pressbooks.library.yorku.ca/masteringzotero/chapter/working-with-tags)  
Platform: [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org)

### 1) Required Research-Type Tag (One Per Entry)

Use one of the following tags per entry:

- `speculative-research`
- `inspirational-research`
- `precedent-research`
- `technical-research`
- `theoretical-research`
- `user-research`

### 2) Optional System Tag (Only If Directly Relevant)

Examples: `Unity`, `Unreal`, `Blender`, `Python`, `Web`, `Hardware`  
Use proper capitalization for platform/proper names.

### 3) Breakpoint Alignment Requirement

For each breakpoint in your **Make It Specific** document, you must:

- have at least one Zotero entry that addresses it,
- be able to filter that entry by research-type tag,
- explain how the research changes your next action.

If research cannot be filtered or cannot be tied to a breakpoint action, it is misaligned.

### Valid Example Entry

- Source: “Gesture Interaction in Small-Space Installations”
- Tags: `technical-research`, `Unity`
- Breakpoint linked: “How to detect stable hand pose in low-light setup?”
- Next step informed: “Test 2 tracking libraries in Unity with low-light video sample.”

## Part II – Practice Presentation (March 9)

This is a practice run of your juried presentation. It is not expected to be polished, but it must be structurally complete and specific.

### Required 10-Minute Structure

Use this flow (adapted from [02-Presentation Deliverables](../../documents-Class/04_Detailed_Speculative_Proposal/02-Presentation_Deliverables.md)):

1. **Introduction (1–2 min)**  
   Project name, one-sentence summary, type, and purpose

2. **Research & Precedents (2–3 min)**  
   Key references, precedent logic, relevant prior work (if applicable)

3. **Project Breakdown (3–4 min)**  
   Climax, PoC target, technical stack, major uncertainties

4. **Milestones & Feasibility (2 min)**  
   GitHub milestones and development strategy

5. **Questions for Feedback (remaining time)**  
   Explicit unknowns, unproven assumptions, scope and feasibility questions

## Part III – Process Diagram (Required in Slides)

Build a flowchart-style process diagram derived from your **Make It Specific** document.

Your diagram must show:

- sequential build steps,
- dependencies,
- where research informs action,
- decomposition of prior breakpoints,
- any remaining uncertainties,
- path to end-of-semester PoC.

If a large leap remains, it is still a breakpoint.

## Part IV – End-of-Semester Roadmap

Create a visual plan from **current state → final presentation**.

Do not treat March 9 or March 16 as final endpoints. They should appear only as intermediate milestones in a longer build arc.

Your roadmap must include:

- major production milestones,
- dependency chains,
- prototype phases,
- testing/iteration cycles,
- risk points and mitigation actions.

Recommended formats:

- Gantt-style timeline (preferred)
- milestone sequencing diagram

Map GitHub issues directly onto this roadmap.
